With music being around for thousands of years it is inevitable that there would be several outstanding musicians, conductors, and composers.

John Philip Sousa

John Philip Sousa was born on November 6, 1854 in the United States, he was a conductor and composer for wind instruments. Most of his work revolves around marches, he intension's were to have a lot of brass instruments to create a loud and hard sound. John was able to give music a twist after the romantic era ended, his music was upbeat and exciting to listen to. He composed pieces such as; The March King, and The Liberty Bell. His music was inspired by the military and their determination to help protect the land.

Anton Reicha

Anton Reicha was a French composer who put his emphasis on woodwind instruments. He was born on February 26, 1770 in Czech Republic. During the classical era most pieces had very light and delicate sound. Anton used this to his advantage by conducting pieces using a lot of woodwind instruments such as his most famous piece No. 2 in E-flat major. This piece fit in well during the time period that he was in and was received well by the public.

Arnold Schoenberg

Arnold Schoenberg was born in Austria on September 1874. He created exciting string music, and his most recognized piece was String Quartet No. 2. He later moved to North America (United States of America) in 1934.
