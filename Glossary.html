Bow:

A bow is a stick with strings attached to it to strum the instrument.

Brass Instruments:

Brass Instruments are created using brass, and use a silver plated mouthpiece. They require wind as well.

Buzz:

It is the action of blowing while your upper lip is over your bottom lip

Classical Era:

Took place during the early 17th century and lasting to the early 18th century. This era brought smoother sounding music, along with a lighter feel to the music.

Composer:

Someone who creates music.

Conductor:

Someone who conducts and ensemble.

Lyre of Ur:

A string instrument similar to a harp.

Music:

Music is a type of sound created by anything.

Plectrum:

A triangular shaped item usually made of plastic or rubber. It is used to prevent your finger from having to strum the strings.

Pluck:

The action of pulling a string and releasing it quickly.

Romantic Era:

Took place between the late 18th century and the early 19th century. Music during this time was more expressive, and emotional.

String Instruments:

String Instruments are instruments which make sound when the strings hit each other.

Wind Instruments:

Use wind or air to create sound

Woodwind Instruments:

Woodwind Instruments use a reed (wood) and require wind to operate.
